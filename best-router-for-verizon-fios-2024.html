    Factors To Consider When Looking for The Best Router For Verizon FiOS

    WiFi Speed & Wireless Protocol

    One of the biggest factors affecting WiFi speed is the wireless protocol built into the device.

    Currently, there are four main wireless protocols that are equipped for modern internet speeds, these are WiFi 5 (802.11ac), WiFi 6 (802.11ax), WiFi 6E (802.11ax), and WiFi 7 (802.11be).

    A WiFi 5 router is capable of handling FiOS internet plans of up to 500 Mbps. These routers are typically more affordable but are less technologically advanced.

    WiFi 6 and WiFi 6E routers best serve FiOS gigabit internet plans and contain advanced features that improve signal precision and multi-device usage.

    WiFi 6E routers also contain an additional 6 GHz frequency band which features extra-wide bandwidth channels of 160 MHz and enables ultra-fast internet speeds of up to 2.5 Gbps.

    WiFi 7 is the latest wireless protocol to be released and offers theoretical internet speeds of up to 40 Gbps.

    This wireless protocol uses multi-link operation which enables devices to connect to multiple frequencies at the same time, and access ultra-wide 320 MHz bandwidth channels.

    Range

    The best third-party routers for a Verizon FiOS network should cover between 2,500 to 3,500 sq. ft and are, therefore, best utilized in larger properties with multiple simultaneous users and devices.

    If the traditional router setup does not adequately cover every corner of your home, you may consider investing in a Verizon FiOS-compatible mesh system.

    Mesh systems best serve large properties and work by connecting a router to multiple nodes which are then placed in strategic locations in the property. The mesh system then creates a unified network with hands-off connections between nodes.

    If you are in a smaller property of 2,500 sq. ft or less, a high-quality router should suffice, and you may consider extending the network using a wireless access point or an extender.

    Do note that the coverage advertised on a router is theoretical and does not consider conditions within your home. Factors such as the home location, internet usage, and obstacles within the home will affect the range.

    Number of Bands

    The WiFi band refers to the specific wireless broadcast frequency. The frequency is split into channels in which a router can send or receive data.

    The bands available on routers are standardized to ensure compatibility across devices and to comply with regional regulations.

    Not all routers use all band types, so a factor to consider is the number of bands the router provides, as more bands mean greater internet connection options.

    The two most common band types are 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz. The higher the band, the higher the data carrying potential and the faster the internet speed (Mbps). However, lower bands have a better range as they can penetrate through walls and large objects.

    Pretty much all routers accommodate 2.4GHz, while most will also have 5GHz. Routers that have two bands are referred to as dual-band routers.

    The latest WiFi 6 protocol also supports a 6GHz band.

    If you have some of the latest smartphones, tablets, or laptops that are 6GHz compatible, you may consider purchasing a router that has 6GHz available (tri-band) as this will maximize the internet speed on your new devices.

    Generally, more bands lead to more options to maximize router performance in your home or office setup.

    Mesh Networks vs Traditional Routers

    A traditional WiFi router has a centralized system while mesh routers adopt a decentralized system. Depending on your home or office setup, you should consider which router type is most suitable.

    The traditional WiFi router will have one unit in one room of a house to where all devices connect to. With this system, there is a greater potential for weaker internet coverage as the network from the single unit may struggle to penetrate through all rooms.

    The Mesh system is designed to overcome this issue. The Mesh system operates using WiFi nodes which accept that a single router cannot be perfectly placed for everyone to achieve a strong internet connection.

    The Mesh system assigns one device as the primary router, while other units are placed strategically across the total space and act as satellites. For the user, the satellites still appear as a seamless, single network.

    Understanding Verizon FiOS Technology

    Fiber optic networks carry light information signals at high speed. The latest technology is close to reaching the speed of light (186,000 miles per second) within the average home internet setup thanks to services such as Verizon FiOS.

    The FiOS is built using cables constructed from hair-like glass strands. These strands send light pulses to transfer data to the home device. A converter transforms the light pulses into electrical impulses that computers require.

    The technology has been popular for a while among businesses that need to easily transfer large datasets. However, the push is increasingly seeing this technology brought to the home internet user.

    In terms of speed, Verizon claims FiOS cables are 25 times faster than conventional cables for loading videos, pictures, and music. Verizon FiOS internet can reach download speeds of 940 Mbps and upload speeds of 880 Mbps, with some locational variances.

    Affordable packages as low as 50 Mbps are also available. This should allow a connected home to manage a network of smart home devices, gaming, and do a lot of streams for play or work.

    The Verizon FiOS technology does not use a modem, but rather an optical network terminal (ONT). The ONT effectively does the work of a modem but operates specifically for fiber optic networks.

    As FiOS uses a fiber optic cable, wired devices such as computers can access the network by plugging the computer directly into the ONT box.

    Verizon FiOS typically performs better in TV streaming and internet speed for high-consumption users, compared to traditional options.

    Also, Verizon’s customer service is outstanding compared to other companies. However, if you are a light internet user, the investment is not worthwhile or particularly different.

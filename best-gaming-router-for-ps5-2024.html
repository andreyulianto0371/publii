    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best Gaming Router For PS5

    WiFi Speed

    One of the main features that can affect the WiFi speed is the WiFi protocol, also known as the WiFi standard. This is a set of standards that devices, satellites, and routers use to communicate.

    WiFi 6E, WiFi 6, and WiFi 5 are the main standards available for gaming routers. Additionally, there is the latest WiFi 7 standard that is used by new gaming routers such as the Netgear Nighthawk RS700S.

    WiFi 5 standard has been available since 2014 and is universally compatible with devices that use the internet. If your internet plan is 500 Mbps and below, a WiFi 5 router is the best option.

    WiFi 6 provides better coverage, faster speeds, and advanced performance features than WiFi 5.

    WiFi 6 gaming routers use WPA3 to provide advanced security to your network and work well when connected to gigabit internet plans.

    To access the advanced features of WiFi 6, you need to use devices that are compatible with WiFi 6. This standard is backward compatible with previous WiFi technologies.

    WiFi 6E is suitable for high-consumption streaming sessions as it comes with a 6 GHz band which helps reduce network latency and congestion and achieve speeds of up to 2.5 Gbps.

    However, WiFi 6E is currently not compatible with PS5 so you should hold off on investing in a WiFi 6E router if you want to improve PS5 performance.

    WiFi 7 is the latest standard and is four times faster than WiFi 6E because it features the 6 GHz band that has 320 MHz wide channel widths to increase capacity and throughput. However, few client devices can operate on the WiFi 7 standard.

    Gaming Features

    The following are the advanced features that enable gaming routers to have reliable internet speeds and zero lags:

    Quality of Service (QoS) ensures that sufficient bandwidth is assigned to a specific task by prioritizing certain connections.

    This feature is essential in households that have multiple connections at the same time, as gaming connections can be prioritized so that they operate without lag.

    MU-MIMO allows multiple devices to connect to the network by providing efficient access to the signal without interference and enabling quick communication with the router.

    OFDMA minimizes bottlenecks in the network when high-bandwidth tasks are running by maximizing the amount of data carriers in a frequency band.

    Beamforming technology enhances the signal precision achieved by the router antennas and reduces the interference level for better reliability and quality of internet connection in your home.

    When it comes to ports, gaming routers should provide a 2.5 Gbps port and multiple 1 Gbps ports to ensure gamers have reduced lag and improved internet speeds by having access to wired connections.

    Number Of Connected Devices

    If your household has multiple gaming devices that need to be connected to the internet, you need to get a gaming router that handles these connections as well as adequately serving the rest of the household.

    Typically, a gaming router should allow for at least 25 simultaneous connections. Some routers can connect up to 100 devices at the same time.

    To get the recommended number of devices a gaming router should support, you should add 10% to 20% on top of the number of devices in your home that need to be connected to the network.

    However, the number of connected devices does not consider the connection types.

    For instance, a gaming router that can be connected to 25 devices does not mean 25 gaming connections at the same time, but rather a mix of low and high-consumption connections.  

    What’s The Difference Between A Gaming Router vs a Regular Router?

    A gaming router is similar to a regular router in terms of set up as it is a device that creates a local WiFi network when connected to a modem.

    Gaming routers are tailored to gaming needs and equipped with gamer-centric optimization and settings to improve gaming performance.

    Some of the features that gaming routers have include Game Accelerator for reducing network latency, 2.5 GB or 1 GB ports for faster-wired connections, and Game Protector dashboard for network monitoring and parental controls.

    Some gaming routers support OneMesh and AiMesh technologies that enable them to create a mesh network throughout your home by increasing coverage. This is necessary especially if your household has multiple gamers in different parts of your home.

    A regular router, especially those given by your ISP is an easy and affordable way of having an internet connection in your home.

    However, these routers provide fewer options for adjusting your network settings and optimizing your network’s performance.

    Regular routers provide dual-band connectivity and enough internet speed to have basic wireless connectivity.

    However, you may experience lag when multiple devices are connected as they may not have features such as Quality of Service that make your gaming experience lag-free and smooth by prioritizing the network bandwidth.

    FAQs

    What Features Should A Gaming Router Have?

    For a reliable gaming experience, a gaming router should be equipped with features like Beamforming for improved signal strength, OFDMA for reduced congestion, MU-MIMO for efficient multi-device connectivity, and QoS for connection prioritization.

    Additionally, it should have multiple gigabit ports for wired gaming connections.

    Should I Get A WiFi 6 or WiFi 6E Router For PS5 Gaming?

    WiFi 6 and WiFi 6E can achieve maximum download speeds of up to 9.6 Gbps. WiFi 6E comes with a new 6GHz band that provides less congestion so that devices compatible with WiFi 6E can take full advantage of the extra bandwidth.

    We recommend not getting a WiFi 6E gaming router for your PS5 as the console does not support the WiFi standard. Therefore, with WiFi 6 you can still enjoy the fastest speeds with minimum lag when gaming.

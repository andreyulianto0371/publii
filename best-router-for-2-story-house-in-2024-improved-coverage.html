    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best Router For A Two Story House

    To ensure that you buy the best router for a 2-story house, you need to consider the following factors:

    House Size & Square Footage

    When considering the best internet setup for a property, the house size and square footage are key factors as they will impact on the type of setup you choose.

    For properties that are smaller than 2,000 sq.ft, we recommend going for a traditional router setup.

    This means your primary network is derived from one high-quality device that can provide network access to multiple users across the property.

    If you find that the single router does not reach every corner of the home, you could invest in a WiFi extender or wireless access point to extend the network.

    A WiFi extender is the cheapest and simplest option, but does take up to 50% of network bandwidth per device.

    A wireless access point provides a more reliable and lower latency connection but needs a wired connection to the main router which can be tricky to setup.

    For properties larger than 2,000 sq.ft, investing in mesh system makes more sense. A mesh system is comprised of a main router and nodes that sync to form a unified network.

    You should place the main router in the center of the property, and then place anywhere between 1 to 4 nodes in locations where the network generally struggles to access.

    WiFi Range & Coverage

    The theoretical WiFi coverage provided by your router should exceed the total square footage of your property.

    We say theoretical because, in reality, the coverage advertised by a manufacturer tends to be an estimate of coverage in perfect conditions.

    The reality is that every home has obstacles and interferences that can restrict the actual coverage of the network.

    To cover a 2 story house, a router should theoretically cover at least 2,000 sq.ft.

    To maximize the coverage of a router within a property you should place it in a central location where there are no immediate obstacles such as thick walls, cupboards or fridges.

    Also, coverage is determined greatly by the frequency band. The 2.4 GHz band offers the best coverage, while the 5 GHz or 6 GHz bands offer the weakest coverage but the highest speed.

    Properties that exceed 2,000 sq.ft may consider investing in a mesh system as they are designed to eliminate dead zones and work best in larger properties.

    WiFi extenders and WAP are also options to extend your coverage if you opt for a traditional router setup.

    Traditional Router vs Mesh System

    Depending on the setup of your home, you can either choose traditional or mesh network routers. A centralized system is seen in traditional routers while a decentralized system is found in mesh systems.

    Traditional routers are placed in one room and all the devices connect to it. Since there is a single unit with traditional routers, there is a high chance that you will experience weaker coverage as the signal may have difficulties penetrating through the walls to all the rooms.

    The mesh system is designed to overcome the above problem as it works using WiFi nodes. The mesh system allocates one device to act as the main router and the other units are strategically located across the house.

    These units act as satellites but they appear as a single, seamless network to the user. Mesh WiFi systems are better for penetrating concrete and brick walls than traditional routers.

    Router Location

    Finding the best place to put your router will ensure that you get good WiFi connectivity. You need to know where you will place your router so that you can get the best signal throughout your house.

    As routers give off signals in all directions, placing them in the corner of a room will ensure that the connection will reach outside the house if you need it.

    To ensure that the WiFi signal reaches all the rooms in your home, you should ideally place it at the center of the house. For a two-story building, you should place the router near the ceiling to ensure that both floors get uninterrupted signals.

    Additionally, you should not put the router near obstructions such as thick walls, electronic devices, or microwaves as it will interrupt your WiFi signal. Metal objects can also disrupt the signal.

    WiFi Speed

    WiFi speed is measured in Mbps, and it is the speed at which you can upload and download files online. Your WiFi speed will be faster if the Mbps is higher.

    Comparing the WiFi speeds of different routers will enable you to find the right one for a two-story house.

    The WiFi speed of your router depends on the bands available which include 5 GHz and 2.4 GHz frequencies. The higher frequencies deliver faster WiFi speeds.

    Some routers have the new 6GHz band which provides internet speeds that are ultra-fast and seamless.

    Additionally, the usage level and the number of users determine the actual speed that you will experience with your router.

    For instance, a router with a WiFi speed of 200 Mbps will be slower when connected to 20 devices but faster when connected to 4 or 5 devices.

    Where To Place Your Router In A Two-Story House

     

    The following are some of the considerations that you need to keep in mind when choosing the right location for your router:

    Consider the floor or ceiling

    For optimal coverage, you should remember the spherical signal dispersion when positioning your router.

    Placing a router on the floor in a basement can result in half of the signals being absorbed by the ground.

    To maximize coverage in a multilevel house, it’s better to position the router on the lower floor’s ceiling or the upper floor’s ground to cover both levels.

    Use a separate router and modem

    While routers combined with modems are convenient for smaller spaces like apartments and small houses, it’s best to purchase a modem and router separately for multilevel houses.

    If your ISP is installed in a location where you cannot get enough signal, you can still use a separate Wi-Fi router even if you have a combined router and modem unit.

    By installing the modem in that location and setting up a dedicated Wi-Fi router in a central location, you can improve internet coverage throughout your home or office.

    Router For A 2-Story House FAQs

    Do I Need A Mesh System For A 2-Story House?

    If you need Wi-Fi coverage for a space larger than 3500 sq. Ft., upgrading to a mesh WiFi router is preferable to using an extender so that you can get better performance.

    While a standalone router can expand its signals to cover a substantial area, a mesh router can provide better coverage and performance for larger spaces.

    Should I Place The Router Downstairs Or Upstairs

    Placing your wireless router on the upper floor is generally better than on the ground floor to minimize potential sources of interference.

    However, this can be challenging, particularly in older homes with unique layouts.

    The ideal placement for your router will vary depending on the layout of your home, your Wi-Fi needs, and the signal strength of your router. If you live in a two-story house, positioning the router in the center of the home is generally optimal.

    What Features Should The Router Have? 

    When purchasing a router, you must take into consideration advanced features such as QoS and MU-MIMO. These features work together to improve network performance.

    Advanced features like MU-MIMO and QoS make it simpler to handle the internet signal for multiple devices connected to a single router.

    MU-MIMO enhances streaming performance by providing a strong, consistent connection for multiple devices on the same router, while QoS helps to ensure reliable and uninterrupted signals for specific devices.

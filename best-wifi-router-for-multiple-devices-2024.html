    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best WiFi Router For Multiple Devices

    WiFi Speed & Wireless ProtocolÂ 

    When looking for a router for multiple devices, considering the WiFi speed and wireless protocol is key.

    Wireless protocol refers to the standardized set of communication protocols that define how a device sends and receives data.

    Devices on the market in 2024 will either provide WiFi 5, WiFi 6, or WiFi 6e, so itâ€™s useful to know the differences.

    WiFi 5 routers are typically cheaper as they are using the older wireless protocol. WiFi 5 routers include the 5 GHz band, MU-MIMO, and beamforming technologies, with theoretical speeds of 1.2 Gbps.

    However, WiFi 5 routers are less likely to reach this threshold, so WiFi 5 routers are best used for lower-speed internet plans of less than 1 Gbps.

    For households with gigabit internet plans, WiFi 6 or WiFi 6e routers are a great choice as they will enable you to maximize the speed on your internet plan.

    These routers will be more premium in price, but the investment will be worth it for the advanced performance.

    WiFi 6 and WiFi 6e routers are either dual-band or tri-band, and provide high-quality WPA3 security, MU-MIMO, and OFDMA technologies.

    The only thing to consider, especially with WiFi 6e routers is that few internet devices currently support this technology, so make sure you have the latest smartphone, TV, or laptop that is WiFi 6e compatible.

    WiFi 7 is the next wireless protocol to be released. The protocol will include tri-bands (2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, and 6 GHz), up to 320 MHz channel bandwidth, multi-link operation, and managed QoS, to deliver the fastest internet speeds of up to 5.8 Gbps.

    WiFiÂ RangeÂ 

    The router you purchase should cover the entire area of your home or office space without needing to buy an extender, therefore, checking the WiFi range is an important step.

    For optimal WiFi range, the router needs to be placed in a central location in the space to support 360-degree coverage.

    When placing the router, you should also ensure there is minimal obstruction in its way, such as concrete walls or cupboards, as these will impact the range.

    Most routers provide a range of up to 2000 sq ft, but some can range up to 5000 sq ft to cover large spaces.

    Routers with large WiFi ranges typically cost more than those with weaker ranges, but the investment is ultimately worth it if it saves you money on extenders.

    If you purchase a router and you discover certain WiFi dead spots in your home or office, you can purchase a WiFi extender, or if youâ€™ve got a router that is mesh-compatible, you can set up the nodes (satellites) in the dead spot areas.

    Advanced FeaturesÂ 

    When looking at the features of a router, you should look out for advanced features such as MU-MIMO, OFDMA, QoS, and Beamforming.

    These are some of the latest WiFi technologies that enable faster and more reliable internet performance.

    Below we explain what each of these technologies do.

    Having MU-MIMO technology is a great addition when finding a router to connect to multiple devices.

    This technology enables simultaneous multi-device communication, which reduces the time needed for a device to wait for a signal, which in turn increases the internet speed.

    Similarly, OFDMA technology supports simultaneous connections by generating many multiple subcarriers within a frequency.

    This process reduces network congestion and ensures a quality, high-speed connection on multiple devices.

    QoS (Quality of Service) allows you to prioritize certain applications and clients on your network.

    For example, if you are a user who is gaming and another is downloading files, implementing the QoS will enable you to prioritize the gaming connection and reduce the lag.

    Beamforming technology improves the quality of WiFi connection by improving the precision of router antennas.

    Beamforming ensures antennas remove interferences and have signals with improved focus.

    Number of BandsÂ 

    A WiFi band is a specific frequency used for data transmission. Most routers are dual-band, offering 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands, while some routers are tri-band and offer an additional 5 GHz band or a 6 GHz band.

    Generally, the more bands available on a router, the better the performance. More bands give you extra bandwidth and added flexibility in terms of which frequency to use.

    Lower frequency bands, such as 2.4 GHz offer slower speeds but better coverage, while the faster band of 5 GHz provides faster speeds but lower coverage.

    WiFi 6e and WiFi 7 devices offer a 6 GHz band. This band offers the fastest internet speeds, but currently, few devices are compatible with this frequency. Tri-band routers are also great for connecting to a mesh network.

    Dual-band routers are cheaper compared to tri-band routers but will satisfy the needs of most households. Tri-band routers are only required for households or workplaces with high internet usage.

    Mesh Systems vs Traditional Routers

    For most households, a traditional router that features some of the latest technologies such as MU-MIMO, OFDMA, and Beamforming, should be sufficient in terms of speed and coverage.

    However, for larger households covering more than 3000 sq ft, we recommend a mesh system to ensure you achieve full home coverage.

    Mesh technology enhances home network coverage by adding satellite devices that are placed strategically within your home.

    A mesh system is more reliable than WiFi extenders as the router and satellites are tuned to create a single network.

    The router and satellites also work together to hand off connections to ensure you always access the fastest WiFi point and enjoy seamless roaming.

    Mesh systems are typically more expensive than purchasing a single router, so only invest if you feel it is worthwhile. In smaller spaces, a modern high-quality router will suffice.

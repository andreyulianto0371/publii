    Things To Consider When Choosing The Best Router For AT&T Fiber

    Speed & Wireless Protocol 

    WiFi speed is measured in Megabits per second (Mbps) and measures the upload and download speed of files.

    Higher Mbps speeds relate to faster WiFi speeds. When looking for the best AT&T fiber-compatible routers, comparing the Mbps is a good first step when comparing router options.

    The available WiFi speed is dependent on the bands. Generally, most routers are dual-band, with 5GHz and 2.4GHz. Higher band frequencies allow for faster WiFi speeds. The new 6GHz band is available in some WiFi 6 routers which offers ultra-fast internet speeds.

    The measure of Mbps is, however, theoretical. The WiFi speed experienced by an individual user is also impacted by the usage type (e.g. surfing vs streaming vs gaming) and the number of users.

    A streamer will have decent streaming quality if they are individually using a router with 50 Mbps. If 5 streamers are simultaneously using the same network, the quality will be much poorer.

    The wireless protocol refers to a set of guidelines and rules for communication. Wireless protocols ensure smooth communication between devices allowing them to interact and connect.

    The wireless protocol that a router uses generates a standard network to enable devices to connect and interact.

    The most known family of protocols is the 802.11 family. The internet speed and capability are enhanced with every protocol upgrade.

    A prime example of this is the 802.11ax, also known as WiFi 6. This is the best-performing protocol as it can accommodate most devices. Not all devices will connect to the WiFi 6 so you should check this before purchasing.

    WiFi Range & Coverage 

    WiFi signals are stronger when there is no object between you and the router and when you are close to the router. When you move further from the router or there are doors and walls between, your connections will degrade.

    High-end routers have longer distance stability due to their higher maximum range. AT&T has a higher maximum range hence better signal strength at home.

    Typically, the range will vary from 2500 to 5000 square feet. You can also boost your WiFi signal using a WiFi extender to improve coverage and limit WiFi dead-ends.

    Ensure that you put your router in a centralized location and on an open shelf.

    Number Of Bands

    The Wifi band relates to the specific wireless broadcast frequency. The frequency is divided into channels that the router accesses to send and receive data.

    There are two standard band options, 5GHz and 2.4GHz. The band with 2.4GHz goes through objects and walls easily while the 5Ghz has faster speed with no competition with household objects.

    There are dual band models and triband models. The dual-band has both 2.4GHz and 5GHz while the triband adds a 6GHz band for ultra-fast speed internet.

    If you have some of the latest tablets, phones, PCs, or laptops, that have 6GHz compatibility, you should consider a tri-band router to maximize the speed potential in your devices.

    Mesh Networks vs Traditional Routers 

    The traditional router sends data from a central location to WiFi or wireless and ethernet devices.

    The mesh WiFi system is designed to overcome the issue of WiFi dead-ends by placing additional devices in spots where the WiFi normally does not reach. The Mesh system uses WiFi nodes which enable a single router to extend its coverage.

    The mesh system identifies one device as a primary router, with the other placed in various locations in a home to act as satellites. No matter where the user is based, the network will appear as one singular network.

    Mesh networks have two to four boxes that are sold together to provide a WiFi signal in your house or business.

    Router Features

    Consumers are most likely to seek features such as beamforming, parental controls, MU-MIMO, and quality of service for potential router options.

    Parental control is a feature that allows you to block devices and websites and also set up schedules. This will give you better control over what your kids can access when using the internet.

    QoS or Quality of Service is a feature that enables you to route your bandwidth to activities that are most important to you.

    This works by pressing a button on the router control panel selecting your prioritized activities such as gaming, streaming, or working.

    Beamforming is ideal for AT&T compatible routers, especially in larger homes or workplaces. This technology maximizes the router signal quality and range.

    MU-MIMO ensures the router can communicate simultaneously with different devices. This feature is ideal for homes or workplaces with many smart devices that maintain a constant connection to the router.

    Understanding AT&T Fiber Technology

    Fiber optic networks use fiber optic cables to transmit and carry light. Compared to coaxial cables, they carry light information signals at high speed.

    Fiber optic cables are considerably thinner than coaxial cables, which allows them to transmit digital data over long distances at great speed.

    Imagine typical cable internet speeds for downloads can vary from 10 to 500 Mbps, AT&T fiber optic improves speed by up to 25 times, with upload and download speeds ranging from 250 to 1000 Mbps.

    To generate the internet connection, AT&T Fiber technology does not use a modem, but rather an optical network terminal (ONT), in combination with a router.

    The ONT effectively operates like a modem but is designed specifically to handle fiber optic networks. The router will then connect to the ONT box to produce the local network within your home or workplace.

    There are two types of AT&T fiber, broadband fiber and direct internet access (DIA) fiber. DIA is typically used by firms that need high-consumption internet to provide a dedicated, uninterrupted connection.

    There are various types of broadband fiber, including fiber to the building (FTTB) and fiber to the home (FTTH). FTTB is used by properties with multiple working and living spaces, while FTTH is used by single households.

    The benefit of AT&T fiber is that the internet speed exceeds the performance capability of regular cable internet, ensuring a fast, reliable, and uninterrupted connection.

    The downsides are that AT&T fiber is not yet accessible in all areas, so you may not be able to access it even if you want to.

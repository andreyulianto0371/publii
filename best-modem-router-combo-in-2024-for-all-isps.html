    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best Modem Router Combo

    WiFi Speed & Coverage

    The speed of your modem router combo must be equal to or higher than the speed of your internet plan.

    However, itâs worth noting that the advertised speed represents the maximum achievable speed under ideal conditions and is not reflective of real-world performance.

    Factors such as network congestion and environmental interference can impact the actual speed experienced by users.

    If your modem router combo is slower than your internet speed, it can act as a bottleneck, limiting the performance of your internet connection.

    On the other hand, expecting speeds higher than what your modem router combo is capable of providing is unrealistic.

    The coverage range of your modem router combo is determined by the setup and location of your home. Obstacles like stairs, doors, or concrete walls can obstruct the wireless signal.

    DOCSIS 3.1 vs DOCSIS 3.0Â 

    DOCSIS, the telecommunications standard used by cable internet providers, offers two options: DOCSIS 3.0 and DOCSIS 3.1. DOCSIS 3.0 is slower but more affordable, while DOCSIS 3.1 is the newer and faster standard.

    DOCSIS 3.1 is around ten times faster than DOCSIS 3.0, and offers greater power efficiency, enhanced security, and lower latency.

    When choosing between the two, several factors come into play. If you have a Gigabit internet plan, most ISPs will require you to use a DOCSIS 3.1 modem.

    Certain ISPs such as Mediacom, require you to use modems with DOCSIS 3.1. The requirement pertains to the modem you are using for your internet connection and may not apply to phone services (VoIP).

    However, the need for a DOCSIS 3.1 modem depends on your specific network setup. If neither of the scenarios mentioned above applies to you, technically you may not require a DOCSIS 3.1 modem.

    Nonetheless, it is advisable to opt for a DOCSIS 3.1 modem as it ensures future proofing for potential infrastructure updates or speed enhancements that may arise.

    Channel Bonding

    Channel bonding can affect the internet speed of your modem router combo as it enables your device to combine multiple channels which increases the amount of traffic it can comfortably support.

    Modem router combos have upstream and downstream channels which enable you to upload and download data. Examples of channel bonding formats include 4*4, 8*4, 16*4, and 32*4.

    A DOCSIS 3.0 combo with 32*8 channel bonding will deliver super-fast WiFi than a combo with 16*4 channel bonding.

    Therefore, if you want to have faster speeds on your network, choose a modem router combo with a higher number of channels.

    Ports

    When choosing a voice modem, itâs essential to consider the number of phone jacks you need. Most voice modems come with two jacks, although there are models that offer only one.

    If you decide to go for a DOCSIS 3.1 voice modem, itâs crucial to pay attention to the Ethernet port. In DOCSIS 3.1 modems, the Ethernet port often becomes the limiting factor for the modemâs bandwidth.

    Some modems offer a Port Bonding feature, which allows you to combine two 1-gigabit ports to achieve a single data stream capable of 2 gigabits.

    Some modems feature a single 2.5-gigabit port instead of two 1-gigabit ports. This configuration ensures compatibility with internet plans offering speeds of up to 2500 Mbps.

    Consequently, such modems are future-proofed and will remain suitable for many years to come.

    Modem Router Combo vs Separate Units

    Deciding whether to get a modem router combo or a separate router and modem depends on your preference and internet needs.

    A modem router combo is simple to troubleshoot, has a compact design that saves space, is user-friendly, and is cost-effective. However, you have less control and limited performance.

    On the other hand, a separate modem and router provide better customization options, better performance and speed, easy network expansion, more security, and compatibility with most ISPs.

    But getting separate units has its drawbacks including difficulty when troubleshooting, the setup can be complex, and upgrading is more expensive.

    Can I Use A Modem Router Combo With Cable Or Fiber Internet?Â 

    You can utilize a modem router combo with cable Internet but not with Fiber.

    Fiber internet technology uses an ONT box which basically replaces the function of a modem, so you only need to buy a WiFi router.

    Fiber-optic technology employs flexible glass strands to transmit data as light, resulting in faster, clearer, and long-distance transmission capabilities. These strands are bundled together and protected by layers of plastic.

    On the other hand, traditional cable technology utilizes coaxial cables to transmit data through electrical signals.

    Coaxial cables consist of a copper core insulated with a copper shield, surrounded by layers of aluminum and plastic.

    Factors To Consider When Looking For A Mesh WiFi System For Xfinity

    WiFi SpeedÂ &Â CoverageÂ 

    One of the factors influencing WiFi speed is the WiFi standard. For mesh systems, the three available WiFi standards are WiFi 5, WiFi 6, and WiFi 6E, with WiFi 7 expected to drop in late 2024.

    WiFi 5 (802.11ac) routers work best on Xfinity internet plans that are 500 Gbps and below.

    As this standard was first brought to market in 2014, it has universal compatibility with internet devices and is affordably priced.

    WiFi 6 (802.11ax) and WiFi 6E are more recent standards and offer technological advances such as a third frequency band that enables ultra-fast internet speeds.

    Mesh systems with these standards are best on Xfinityâ€™s gigabit internet plans and are typically more expensive.

    Mesh systems should deliver coverage of 2500 to 5500 sq ft. Do note that lower frequency bands (2.4 GHz) far exceed the coverage of higher frequency bands (5 GHz and 6 GHz), even with mesh setups.

    The coverage you need is dependent on the size of your home. Luckily with mesh systems, you can buy additional nodes to add to your setup in case the initial purchase package didnâ€™t cover the whole home.

    Features

    The best quality mesh WiFi systems for Xfinity offer advanced features such as MU-MIMO, OFDMA, and Beamforming. We explain what these features offer below.

    MU-MIMO is perfect for enabling quality network connection when your household has many internet-using devices connected at the same time.

    The technology does this by ensuring devices can quickly communicate with the router and gain signal access without interruption, thereby improving internet speeds.

    OFDMA reduces network congestion by increasing the amount of data carriers within a frequency channel.

    The technology works to prevent bottlenecks and ensures users engage in high-consumption internet activities (gaming, streaming, etc) without latency issues.

    Beamforming technology focuses on the router antennas. The technology is designed to improve the precision of the signal while minimizing interference.

    Having a more precise network signal in turn leads to faster and more reliable internet throughout the home.

    OFDMA increases the number of data carriers within a frequency channel to reduce congestion on the network.

    Number Of Connected DevicesÂ 

    Most modern-day households have many devices requiring an internet connection, often at the same time.

    The best mesh WiFi router for Xfinity offer up to 25 connected devices, but some products offer more than 100 connected devices if that is your preference.

    When considering the mesh WiFi system for your home setup, we suggest you first count the total number of internet-using devices in the household.

    From this figure, we propose you add 10% to 20% on top to reveal the ideal number of connected devices you will need your mesh WiFi system to deliver.

    Do be aware though that these figures are somewhat theoretical in that they do not consider the activities that users are doing.

    A mesh WiFi system that can deliver 25 connected devices, assumes there will be a range of simultaneous usage types. It wonâ€™t be able to handle 25 high-consumption gaming connections.

    WiFiÂ Mesh Systems vs WiFi ExtendersÂ 

    When working out if you want a mesh WiFi system or WiFi extenders, the main trade-off is between the benefits of WiFi coverage and speed offered by mesh, compared to cheaper cost, easier setup, and reduced energy consumption provided by WiFi extenders.

    If you want a quick and cheap way of extending your current WiFi, a WiFi extender is the best way to go. WiFi extenders tend to be small devices that are plugged into a power source.

    The network that is recast by extenders requires a separate login by users. Also, WiFi extenders use up to 50% total network bandwidth per extender.

    Mesh WiFi systems are more sophisticated than WiFi extenders.

    To install a mesh system, you will need to remove your existing setup, connect the mesh router to your Xfinity compatible modem, strategically place the nodes within the home, and connect them to the router.

    Once set up, a mesh WiFi system exceeds the performance of WiFi extenders because it establishes a unified network, requiring no additional logins.

    Through AI technology, mesh systems also hand off connections between the router and nodes to ensure users are accessing the clearest WiFi access point.

    Given the improved performance and features, mesh systems are more expensive. Also, they are more energy-consuming than extenders as typical mesh setups include at least three devices permanently consuming energy. Â

    Does a Mesh WiFi System Replace A Router?Â 

    Mesh systems are indeed designed to replace an existing router. Mesh systems include a router and nodes (satellites) which are then placed throughout the home to improve WiFi coverage through a unified network.

    If you donâ€™t want to get rid of your existing setup but just want to extend the network, a WiFi extender is a more appropriate choice.

    Are Mesh WiFi Systems Compatible With Xfinity?Â 

    Mesh WiFi systems are compatible with Xfinity, however, you will also need to have a modem that is compatible with Xfinity to ensure the system runs effectively.

    To achieve maximum performance, we recommend you align the mesh WiFi standard with the speed of your internet plan so that you can enjoy the benefits of the ultra-fast Xfinity network.

    Do I Need A Separate Modem?Â 

    To install a mesh WiFi system on your Xfinity internet plan you will need a separate modem that is compatible with Xfinity. Â 

    If you own the Xfinity gateway, you can put the device into bridge mode to then connect to the mesh WiFi router and set up the mesh network.

    Does Mesh WiFi Reduce Or Increase Speed?Â 

    Mesh WiFi systems do not increase or decrease the internet speed within your home, rather they will extend the coverage and maintain existing speeds.

    Due to the syncing between routers and nodes, mesh systems transmit signals further and more consistently compared to WiFi extenders and are therefore the best means of maintaining existing internet speeds in every corner of the home.Â

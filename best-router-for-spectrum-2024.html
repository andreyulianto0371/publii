    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best Router For Spectrum

    When you are looking for the best Spectrum compatible router, you need to consider various factors. We explore these factors in the section below.

    WiFi Speed & Wireless Protocol

    To ensure you maximize the speed of your Spectrum internet plan, you need to align the speed available with the wireless protocol of the third-party router.

    The different wireless protocols include WiFi 5, WiFi 6, WiFi 6E, and the latest WiFi 7. If your internet plan is around 500 Mbps or less, getting a router with WiFi 5 is a good choice. For Gigabit internet plans, a router with WiFi 6 technology is the perfect option.

    WiFi 6E routers can handle internet speeds that are over 1000 Mbps but are not backward compatible with previous standards.

    WiFi 7 is the latest wireless protocol and is four times faster than WiFi 6E and WiFi 6, and therefore operates best high-speed gigabit internet plans.

    A WiFi 7 router will however be very expensive and there are not many WiFi 7 compatible client devices available, given the protocol’s recent release.

    To make your search easier, find out the wireless standard you will require before buying a router.

    Range & Signal Strength

    The total area where the internet signal can be accessed from a given point refers to the WiFi range. Most routers that are designed for small workplaces or households cover a range of between 2500 to 3000 square feet.

    Your home setup and location will determine the range of your router. For instance, if the space between the user and the router is covered by stairs, doors, and walls, you will likely experience weaker coverage compared to a setup that is single-level and open.

    You can boost your router if the range is weaker using an extender. This is a common strategy for many individuals who want to remove WiFi dead spots.

    However, the technological enhancements in the latest routers that use WiFi 6 protocol remove the need for extenders which improves the WiFi range.

    For example, the latest routers feature beamforming technology and high-gain antennas to improve the range.

    Additionally, routers with mesh WiFi are specifically designed to remove any dead zones by improving the internet range.

    Number of Bands

    Considering the band you are planning to use is important when purchasing a router. Most households either use the 5 GHz or the 2.4 GHz band.

    The 2.4 GHz band travels better through doors and walls and has a better range. However, it is prone to interference from other devices and has a maximum speed of around 150 Mbps.

    The 5 GHz band can achieve faster speeds but is not too good with obstacles or walls and can’t travel as far.

    The 6 GHz band is the latest and is available with WiFi 6E and WiFi 7 routers only.

    The 6 GHz band contains a wider channel width of 160 MHz (WiFi 6E) or ultra-wide 320 MHz channels with WiFi 7. This band is capable of reducing congestion and achieving faster speeds than the 5 GHz band.

    Mesh Networks vs Traditional Routers

    Mesh routers use a decentralized system while traditional routers use a centralized system. You should buy a router type that is suitable according to your office or home setup.

    With a traditional router, all the devices connect to one unit located in one room of your home.

    Since the network from a single unit may have difficulties penetrating through all the rooms, there is a higher chance that this system will have weaker internet coverage.

    Therefore, the mesh system is designed to solve this problem. It works using WiFi nodes to achieve a strong internet connection throughout the house.

    This system operates by assigning one device which works as the primary router and the other units are located across the entire space strategically to act as satellites that as a single, seamless network for the user.

    Router Features 

    Below are some of the additional features of routers that you need to consider when reviewing the different options.

    QoS or Quality of Services enables your router to prioritize data on your network. For the QoS to work, you need to press the button on the control panel and choose the activities you want to prioritize such as gaming, streaming, or working from home.

    Parental control enables parents to control and monitor their kid’s activities on the internet. This is essential for parents with younger children as they can block URLs and topics when the internet is accessible.

    A guest network allows you to create an additional network with limited access. This increases security as guests cannot share the same network as your devices.

    Beamforming is suitable if your home is large as it improves the range and quality of the router signal.

    MU-MIMO improves the ability of the router to talk to several devices at the same time. If your home has many devices that constantly communicate with the router, a router with MU-MIMO technology is a good option.

    Separate Units vs Modem Router Combo For Spectrum Internet 

    1. Form-Factor

    Modem router combos have a more compact design since they remove the need for two separate devices and cables to connect them. As a result, a modem router combo occupies less space compared to individual devices.

    2. Set-Up

    It is easy to set up a modern router combo. Instead of dealing with two separate devices to power, connect, and run cables between them, you only need to connect and set up a single device.

    Modem router combo devices are particularly beginner-friendly during the initial set-up process, which involves adjusting settings, configuring passwords, and connecting multiple devices.

    3. Router Placement

    Using a separate router enables you to position it farther from the modem. Individual devices give you greater flexibility to position the router for optimal Wi-Fi signal strength in the areas you need it most.

    The placement of a modem router combo is limited due to the constraints of the coax cable used to connect it.

    This cable is often thicker and difficult to route across a room, which can restrict the positioning of the modem router combo away from the coax cable outlet.

    4. Price Comparison

    An individual router can be similarly priced compared to a modem router combo. Although the initial cost of purchasing separate modem and router devices is higher than a combined unit, it can ultimately be a more cost-effective option in the long term.

    Using a separate modem and router devices gives you the freedom to upgrade them individually, which can save you money in the long run.

    With a modem router combo, you would need to replace the entire unit to make upgrades or address any unfixable issues.

    However, if you purchase the devices separately, you can upgrade them separately and enjoy new features as they become available.

    Moreover, modems often last longer than routers, so combining them can restrict your ability to upgrade and optimize your network over time.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Are any routers compatible with Spectrum?

    Technically the answer is yes. The modem is the key device as this is what connects directly to the Spectrum network.

    Assuming that your router has a WAN port to connect to the modem, then pretty much all routers are compatible with Spectrum.

    Can I use any router with Spectrum?

    Yes, it’s possible and advisable to use your third-party router instead of the router provided by your ISP. ISP-provided routers may have outdated features and limitations that can result in lower speeds and coverage.

    However, before connecting your router to the ISP gateway, it is important to disable the wireless feature on the ISP router to avoid any conflicts or interference with your own router’s signal.

    Should you get a router or a modem & router combination?

    While modem router combos offer simplicity and affordability, purchasing a separate modem and router can provide superior performance and a wider selection of devices to choose from.

    Generally, a separate modem and router are recommended for Spectrum. However, if you have a smaller home and are using Spectrum’s 300 Mbps Internet plan, a combined modem router device may be sufficient.

    Keeping your modem and router separate provides an additional level of control that can be beneficial. Using separate devices allows for greater flexibility in terms of upgrading and optimizing your network.

    The benefits of using an individual router include faster speeds and a wider range of options to choose from.

    On the other hand, modem router combinations offer ease of setup and are typically more affordable. There is less equipment to purchase, and less setup required for modem router combo.

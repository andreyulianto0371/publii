    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best Mesh WiFi System For Gaming

    WiFi Speed & Coverage 

    One of the main features impacting speed and coverage is the WiFi standard (also known as WiFi protocol).

    This refers to a set of standards through which routers, satellites, and devices communicate.

    The three available standards for mesh WiFi systems are WiFi 5, WiFi 6, and WiFi 6E. WiFi 7 mesh systems such as the Netgear Orbi 970 are now available to purchase, but there are very few client devices available that you can use to utilize the WiFi 7 standard.

    WiFi 5 (802.11ac) is universally compatible with internet-using devices given that it has been available since 2014. WiFi 5 mesh systems work best on internet plans of 500 Gbps and below.

    WiFi 6 (802.11ax) offers advanced performance features, faster speeds, and better coverage compared to WiFi 5.

    WiFi 6 mesh systems operate well on gigabit internet plans and through WPA3 have advanced security to protect your network.

    WiFi 6 systems are also backward compatible with previous standards, but to gain access to the advanced features, you will need devices that are WiFi 6 compatible.

    WiFi 6E is an extension of WiFi 6 but includes a 6 GHz frequency band to enable WiFi speeds of up to 2.5 Gbps and also reduce network congestion and latency which is ideal for intense online gaming sessions.

    WiFi 7 (802.11be) routers are now available but very few client devices can operate on the standard.

    In terms of gaming, WiFi 7 is expected to offer a four-fold increase in WiFi speed compared to WiFi 6E. These higher speeds are enabled by 320 MHz wide channel widths in the 6 GHz frequency band, which increase throughput and capacity.

    In terms of coverage, mesh systems generally offer between 2,500 to 7,500 sq ft. For the investment in mesh to be worthwhile, we advise it is deployed in a home with a minimum of 2,000 sq ft.

    Also, note that coverage is variable based on the frequency band used. The 2.4 GHz band offers the best coverage but the lowest speeds, while the 6 GHz band on WiFi 6E systems offers the weakest coverage but fastest speeds.

    Features 

    The best mesh WiFi systems for gaming offer advanced features that enable zero lag and reliable internet speeds. We explain these features below.

    QoS (Quality of Service) is an important feature for gamers as it allows users to prioritize certain connections to ensure sufficient bandwidth is allocated to a given task.

    In a household with multiple simultaneous connections, QoS can be enacted to prioritize a gaming connection to allow it to run without lag.

    MU-MIMO also supports multi-device connectivity by ensuring devices can quickly communicate with the router and gain signal access efficiently without any interference.

    OFDMA is ideal for gamers as it reduces bottlenecks in the network when high-consumption activities are running. It does this by increasing the amount of data carriers within a frequency.

    Beamforming technology improves the precision of the signal achieved by the router antennas and minimizes the level of interference. Improved network signal increases the quality and reliability of internet connection at home.

    In terms of ports, mesh WiFi systems for gaming should offer multiple 1 Gbps ports and ideally a 2.5 Gbps port, to ensure gamers can have access to wired connections which improves internet speeds and reduces lag.   

    Number Of Connected Devices 

    Modern households have many devices connected to the internet at the same time. You therefore need to invest in a mesh WiFi system that can handle a large volume of simultaneous connections without affecting performance.

    Mesh WiFi systems typically offer a minimum of 25 connected devices, with some systems claiming over 100 simultaneous connections.

    We suggest you add 10% to 20% on top of the total number of devices you have at home, to give you a recommended figure for the number of connected devices you need from your mesh system.

    A good point to note though is that the number of connected devices does not consider the type of connections.

    For example, a mesh system offering 25 connected devices does not mean 25 simultaneous gaming connections, but rather a range of connections that are mainly low-consumption.

    WiFi Mesh Systems vs Gaming Routers 

    WiFi mesh systems ensure you can maintain your existing internet speed throughout the home, through the connection of a router to multiple nodes.

    The router and nodes sync to create a unified network, meaning that users do not have to separately login to different internet connections.

    Also, the devices work in tandem to hand off connections to ensure users are always connected to the strongest WiFi access point based on their location within the home.

    A gaming router resembles a traditional router setup in that it is a single device that is connected to a modem and then creates the local WiFi network.

    However, certain routers are tailored for gamers by having gamer-centric settings and optimizations to maximize gaming performance.

    These features include having many 1 GB or 2.5 GB ports, Game Accelerator settings to reduce the network latency, and a Game Protector dashboard to allow parental controls and network monitoring.

    FAQs

    Are Mesh WiFi Systems Good For Gaming?

    Due to the multiple WiFi access points, mesh WiFi systems have increased in popularity with gamers due to their ability to offer no lag and minimal latency when gaming.

    This setup often performs better than a traditional router setup where the bandwidth of one device is being used by an entire household, which creates a crowded network and a lagged gaming performance.

    What Features Should A Gaming Mesh WiFi System Have? 

    To ensure reliable gaming performance, a gaming mesh WiFi system should have advanced features such as QoS which enables connection prioritization, MU-MIMO which supports efficient multi-device connectivity, OFDMA which reduces network congestion and Beamforming which improves signal strength.

    Also, to allow wired gaming connections, there should be multiple gigabit ports available in the mesh setup.

    Is WiFi 7 Mesh System Good For Gaming? 

    Although WiFi 7 routers are now available, there are very few WiFi 7 client devices available that can utilize WiFi 7 benefits.

    However, when available WiFi 7 offers gamers a superior gaming experience through features such as the High Band Simultaneous Multi-Link, while also providing incredible coverage.

    The HBS Multi-Link feature enables high throughput and minimal lag by shifting latency-critical applications to the higher frequency bands, which in turn reduces traffic on the 2.4 GHz band.

    These advanced features will maximize performance for XR/AR gaming experiences.

    What Brands Make The Best Mesh WiFi Systems For Gaming? 

    As seen in our product reviews, the top brands to look out for when identifying a mesh WiFi system for gaming include ASUS, TP-Link, Netgear, and Amazon Eero.

    While other brands are also available, from our product testing, these brands offered the best performance.

    Do You Lose Speed With Mesh WiFi? 

    Mesh systems are designed to maintain existing WiFi speeds and increase the coverage of that speed to every corner of the home.

    Mesh systems are therefore the best means of extending your network, especially for those on high-speed internet plans.

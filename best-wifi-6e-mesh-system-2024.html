    Factors To Consider When Looking For The Best WiFi 6E Mesh Router

    WiFi Speed 

    One of the primary reasons for investing in a WiFi 6E mesh system is to achieve ultra-fast internet speeds.

    Below we outline some key differences between the standards of WiFi 6, WiFi 6E, and the latest WiFi 7, so you can understand how they vary in terms of WiFi speed.

    WiFi 6 (802.11ax) was launched in 2019 and is adopted across the globe, with the estimated value of the market for WiFi 6 products at USD 20 million.

    WiFi 6 standard mesh systems can handle gigabit internet plans, with top speeds of 1.2 Gbps and a maximum theoretical throughput of 9.6 Gbps.

    WiFi 6E goes beyond the capability of WiFi 6 by creating a ‘fast lane’ through the addition of the 6 GHz frequency band, handling speeds of up to 2.5 Gbps.

    The 6 GHz frequency band provides more than twice the bandwidth offered by the 5 GHz band, which in turn reduces congestion and interference on the network, enabling optimal performance.

    Unlike WiFi 6, WiFi 6E systems are not backward compatible with earlier standards, therefore you will need to ensure your client devices are compatible with WiFi 6E.

    WiFi 7 (802.11be) mesh WiFi systems are the latest to be released and offer incredibly fast internet speeds of up to 10 Gbps and throughput of 46 Gbps.

    The ultra-fast speeds are enabled by doubling the bandwidth channels to 320 MHz which in turn reduces network congestion.

    Currently, there are not many WiFi 7 client devices available, but WiFi 7 routers and mesh systems are backward compatible with WiFi 5 and WiFi 6.

    WiFi Coverage 

    Mesh systems offer seamless internet access throughout the home, with users required to log in to the network once.

    If you’re looking to invest in a mesh WiFi system, one of your main considerations will be the coverage offered by the system.

    Most mesh WiFi systems will provide coverage between 2,000 to 5,500 sq. ft, however, WiFi 6E mesh systems offer a minimum coverage of 6,000 sq. ft.

    Mesh systems are generally most worthwhile in larger properties of at least 2,000 sq. ft, where there are issues with WiFi dead zones. The coverage of a mesh system can be boosted by purchasing an additional node.

    When reviewing the coverage offered by a mesh WiFi system, you should note that the figures presented are theoretical, in that they do not consider the nuances of your home.

    For example, if your home is located in a rural area with a weak internet signal, or if you have thick walls or objects that interfere with the internet connection, the coverage will ultimately be impacted.

    Do note that the mesh WiFi system coverage is most impacted by the frequency band. For WiFi 6E mesh systems, the coverage from the 6 GHz band will be the weakest, while the coverage from the 2.4 GHz band is the strongest.

    Number Of Supported Devices 

    Given the nature of modern households where most individuals have multiple client devices, reviewing the number of supported devices is crucial to ensure the mesh system will handle the needs of your household.

    WiFi 6E mesh systems should offer a minimum of 100 supported devices, but some systems can handle more than 200 devices.

    These figures are also theoretical in that they do not consider the type of internet user.

    For example, a system offering 100 supported devices is not assuming 100 simultaneous streaming connections, but rather a range of different internet connections.

    To work out the optimal number of supported devices needed by your household, total the number of client devices you currently have and add a 20% premium to reach the optimal figure.

    Mesh Systems vs WiFi Routers 

    The debate as to whether you need a mesh WiFi system or a traditional WiFi router setup is dependent on your circumstances and household internet patterns.

    Given mesh WiFi systems are a significant investment, we recommend mesh WiFi systems are most worth the investment for properties that are a minimum of 2,000 sq. ft with many simultaneous internet users and devices.

    Also, if you haven’t had a mesh system before, the setup requires a bit more thought than a single router, as you will need to set the router, sync the nodes, and strategically place the nodes in optimal locations in the property.

    Assuming you have a quality router, a traditional WiFi router setup where there is one device, connected to a modem, or a single modem-router combo unit, will suffice for the majority of households residing in smaller-sized properties.

    This is especially true for WiFi 6E routers, which are designed with the latest technology features such as MU-MIMO, Beamforming, and OFDMA, which reduce congestion, increase speed, and improve signal precision.

    The network of a single WiFi router can also be extended using a WiFi extender, which is a fraction of the cost of a mesh system.

    FAQs

    Does a Mesh WiFi System Replace A Router? 

    Mesh systems will require you to replace your existing router with the mesh router which then connects to additional nodes (satellites) which are located across the property.

    If you are generally happy with your existing router but want to extend the coverage, you may consider opting for a WiFi extender rather than a mesh WiFi system.

    How Much Faster Is WiFi 6E vs WiFi 6? 

    Although both WiFi 6E and WiFi 6 can handle gigabit internet plans, WiFi 6E can theoretically handle speeds of up to 2.5 Gbps, while WiFi 6 tops at 1.2 Gbps.

    This means that the speed capability of WiFi 6E is approximately double that of WiFi 6.

    This difference in speed is enabled through the 6 GHz band included with WiFi 6E which provides additional bandwidth.

    Do Mesh WiFi Systems Have Ethernet Ports? 

    Mesh systems do have ethernet ports to enable wired connections. Some mesh WiFi systems will have ethernet ports on the router device only, while others will have ports on the router and nodes.

    If your household needs multiple wired connections, look at products where ethernet ports are available across mesh units.

    Does Mesh WiFi Reduce Or Increase Speed? 

    Mesh systems do not reduce or increase speed but rather maintain the existing network speed and boost it to every corner of the home.

    Increasing internet speeds needs either a review of your internet plan from the ISP or an investment in a router that is capable of maximizing the speed on your plan.

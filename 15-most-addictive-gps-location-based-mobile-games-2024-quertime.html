    15 Most Addictive GPS Location-based Mobile Games (2024) - Quertime

    Andre Yulianto January 25, 2024

    Do you remember that snake game in the Nokia mobiles? This is what now the GPS location-based smartphone games have made us. We all have become that snake who wander around just to get his reward. Moreover, no one knows why people are getting addictive to the location based games and why it is providing satisfaction to the certain degree. However, location-based games niche are still very small as compare to the other niches of mobile gaming but due to the involvement of people in these games, they have become the new trend in mobile gaming.

    We all know about the success of the Pokémon Go. However, there are many other games too which is providing the same fun and excitement that Pokémon Go did. The app stores of Google and Apple has many GPS location based that have even more features than many famous games. However, due to no or low marketing, these games are still not known by the people as they should be. That is why I decided to write an article in which I will introduce my readers to the 20 extremely addicting GPS location based mobile games which will change your taste in gaming.

    1. Pokémon GO

    Pokémon GO is an augmented reality (AR) mobile game that revolutionized the gaming industry by merging the virtual and real worlds. Developed by Niantic in collaboration with Nintendo and The Pokémon Company, the game encourages players to explore their surroundings to find and catch Pokémon. Utilizing GPS technology, players can discover Pokémon in various locations, participate in virtual battles, and join community events. The game promotes outdoor activity, social interaction, and teamwork, fostering a sense of adventure as players embark on quests and challenges.

    2. Ingress Prime

    Created by Niantic, Ingress Prime is an augmented reality (AR) game that revolves around the conflict between two factions, the Enlightened and the Resistance. Players explore real-world locations to capture portals, link them to form control fields, and engage in strategic battles with the opposing faction. The game encourages social interaction, teamwork, and exploration, turning the entire world into a virtual battlefield. Ingress Prime is known for its complex narrative, community-driven events, and its influence on the development of other location-based games.

    3. Jurassic World Alive

    Jurassic World Alive, developed by Ludia, brings dinosaurs into the real world through augmented reality. Utilizing GPS technology, players explore their surroundings to discover and collect various dinosaur species. The game allows players to create and enhance their own dinosaurs, engage in battles with other players, and participate in special events. With realistic visuals and the integration of location-based features, Jurassic World Alive provides an immersive experience, blending the excitement of dinosaur encounters with strategic gameplay.

    4. Landlord

    Landlord transforms the real world into a virtual property market. Players can buy, sell, and trade virtual properties based on their actual locations, utilizing GPS technology. As users navigate their cities, they encounter opportunities to acquire famous landmarks, businesses, and iconic locations. The game fosters strategic thinking and business acumen, encouraging players to build a virtual real estate empire. With a social aspect, players can interact, negotiate, and compete with others, making it a dynamic and immersive experience that merges gaming with the real world.

    5. Geocaching

    Geocaching is a pioneer in the world of location-based games. Founded on the principle of high-tech treasure hunting, geocachers use GPS coordinates to locate hidden containers, or “geocaches,” placed by other players around the globe. Groundspeak Inc. developed the platform, fostering a global community of explorers. Geocaching encourages outdoor adventure, as players embark on a quest to discover hidden treasures in diverse environments. With millions of geocaches worldwide, the game emphasizes exploration, discovery, and the joy of uncovering hidden gems in unexpected places.

    6. Zombie, Run!

    Zombie, Run! transforms jogging into a thrilling, immersive adventure. This GPS location-based mobile game combines fitness with survival horror. Players assume the role of a survivor in a post-apocalyptic world overrun by zombies. The game motivates users to run by weaving an interactive narrative into their workout. As participants jog, they encounter virtual zombie hordes and must outrun them to gather essential supplies. With real-world surroundings enhancing the eerie atmosphere, Zombie, Run! seamlessly integrates gameplay and exercise, offering an innovative approach to fitness apps.

    7. Pikmin Bloom

    Pikmin Bloom seamlessly blends the whimsical world of Pikmin with augmented reality and GPS technology. Developed by Niantic in collaboration with Nintendo, the game transforms the real world into a garden filled with adorable Pikmin creatures. Players use their smartphones to explore their surroundings, planting virtual flowers and collecting Pikmin. The GPS functionality enhances the gaming experience, encouraging outdoor exploration as players cultivate a vibrant garden. As players travel, the Pikmin accompany them, fostering a sense of companionship and ecological stewardship. The game’s emphasis on real-world interaction and growth creates a unique and enchanting mobile gaming experience.

    8. Dragon Quest Walk

    Dragon Quest Walk is another location-based game developed by Square Enix. Drawing inspiration from the popular Dragon Quest series, the game invites players to embark on a quest to save the world by exploring their surroundings. The real-world map becomes the backdrop for epic battles, quests, and the discovery of iconic Dragon Quest monsters. With AR features, players can see monsters and characters from the beloved franchise in their own environments, creating a magical and immersive experience that bridges the gap between fiction and reality.

    9. Resources Games

    Resources Game blends strategy and exploration. Set in a virtual world parallel to the real one, players embark on a quest to discover and exploit various resources scattered across their geographical locations. The game uses GPS technology to generate a map that mirrors the player’s surroundings, making the exploration truly immersive. As players gather resources, they can trade, collaborate, and compete with others, adding a social and strategic layer to the gameplay. With its unique integration of real-world geography and resource management, Resources Game provides an engaging experience that encourages players to explore their surroundings in a novel and interactive way.

    10. Orna: The GPS RPG

    Orna: The GPS RPG leverages GPS technology to create a vast, virtual world intertwined with reality. Developed by Cutlass Software, the game invites players to embark on a quest as they explore their surroundings. The GPS system determines in-game locations, dungeons, and encounters, making every journey an epic adventure. With a deep class system, real-time battles, and a player-driven economy, “Orna” brings traditional RPG elements into the real world, encouraging outdoor exploration and collaboration. The game’s integration of GPS adds a layer of depth to the gameplay, offering a unique and immersive experience for fantasy RPG enthusiasts.

    11. City Domination

    City Domination is a strategic, location-based mobile game that transforms real cities into virtual battlegrounds. Players compete for dominance by claiming and managing virtual territories in their actual geographical locations. This game fosters a dynamic blend of strategy, social interaction, and exploration as users engage in territorial battles with rivals. With a global player base, City Domination bridges the virtual and physical worlds, offering a unique gaming experience that requires both strategic thinking and real-world exploration. Whether collaborating with allies or challenging opponents, players strive to become the ultimate ruler of their city in this engaging mobile strategy game.

    12. Magic Streets: GPS Realm Game

    Magic Streets transforms the real world into a magical realm. Developed by Shadow Masters, the game encourages players to explore their surroundings to collect resources, cast spells, and engage in battles with other players. The gameplay is heavily influenced by the player’s location, with different areas offering distinct resources and challenges. Magic Streets aims to provide a magical and immersive experience as players navigate their neighborhoods, discover hidden treasures, and interact with a virtual world overlaid on the real one. The game combines elements of exploration, strategy, and social interaction to create a captivating and enchanting gaming experience.

    13. Wokamon

    Wokamon combines fitness with gaming by turning physical activity into a captivating adventure. The game encourages players to walk, run, or exercise to hatch and collect adorable virtual creatures called Wokamons. Each step contributes to the well-being and growth of these virtual companions, creating an innovative approach to fitness tracking. With a charming design and a sense of companionship, Wokamon motivates players to lead an active lifestyle while fostering a connection with their virtual pets. The game leverages GPS tracking to incorporate real-world movements into the gameplay, promoting health and well-being in an entertaining way.

    14. Turf Wars

    Turf Wars is an exhilarating GPS-based mobile game that combines strategy and real-world exploration. Developed by MeanFreePath, the game turns your city into a digital battleground where players vie for control over territories. Using GPS, players physically move around to claim and defend areas, engaging in virtual turf battles with other players. The game fosters community engagement as players form alliances and compete for dominance in their local neighborhoods. With its dynamic gameplay and social interaction, Turf Wars transforms the urban landscape into a competitive playground, providing an immersive and strategic gaming experience.

    15. The Walk

    The Walk is a compelling fitness-focused GPS location-based mobile game that transforms walking into an epic adventure. Developed by the creators of Zombies, Run!, The Walk presents a gripping narrative where players become the protagonist in a suspenseful story. To progress in the game, players must physically walk certain distances, unlocking the next chapter of the narrative. The integration of GPS technology ensures that the story unfolds based on real-world movement. With its immersive storytelling and fitness motivation, The Walk encourages players to achieve their daily step goals while experiencing a captivating storyline, effectively merging the realms of gaming and exercise.

    16. Revenge of the Gang

    Revenge of the Gang is an action-packed GPS location-based mobile game that combines augmented reality (AR) with real-world locations. Set in a virtual city, players become part of a gang, engaging in battles, heists, and challenges that unfold in their actual surroundings. The game leverages GPS to synchronize virtual elements with the player’s physical environment, creating an immersive experience. Players can form alliances, strategize attacks, and conquer territory in a dynamic and competitive multiplayer setting. With its unique blend of AR, GPS technology, and gang warfare gameplay, Revenge of the Gang offers an adrenaline-pumping experience that blurs the lines between the virtual and real world.

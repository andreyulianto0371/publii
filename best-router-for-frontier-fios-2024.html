    Things To Consider When Choosing The Best Frontier FiOS Compatible Routers

    WiFi Speed & Wireless Protocol

    The first thing that you need to consider when buying a Frontier FiOS-compatible router is the speed that comes with your FiOS plan.

    The majority of households are comfortable using the Fiber 500 plan which provides enough internet speed for a whole family even if a couple of individuals are doing intense gaming sessions simultaneously.

    However, if you want to increase the performance of your router and increase the speed even faster, you can get the 1 Gig plan.

    In this case, you should check the top speed of any potential router that you want to get to ensure see the benefits of the plan.

    Additionally, the speed on your router is dependent on the bands. Most routers have dual bands, that is, 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz. Some WiFi 6 routers have the new 6 GHz bands for ultra-fast speeds.

    The set of rules and guidelines for router communication is known as the wireless protocol. It ensures that the devices connect and interact using a standard network so that they can have smooth communication.

    The most common wireless protocol is 802.11. When there is a protocol upgrade, the internet capability and speed are improved. For example, the 802.11ax wireless protocol performs better as it can connect to many devices.

    Range & Coverage

    The range of a WiFi signal represents the geographical area around an access point where users can connect to the internet.

    Generally, routers intended for homes or small offices use a coverage area of around 2500 to 3000 square feet for optimal performance.

    The coverage of a router is also influenced by the location and setup of the home. When obstacles like walls, doors, and stairs obstruct the path between the router and the user, the signal strength may weaken, resulting in reduced coverage.

    Conversely, an open, single-level setup may provide better coverage.

    Many households use extenders as a common strategy to eliminate WiFi dead zones caused by routers with weak ranges. Extenders can help boost the signal and improve coverage.

    With the introduction of the WiFi 6 protocol, the latest routers aim to eliminate the need for extenders by enhancing the technology to improve the WiFi range.

    High-gain antennas and Beamforming technology are among the enhancements implemented in these routers to extend the coverage.

    Mesh WiFi routers are engineered to optimize internet range and eliminate dead zones by utilizing multiple units spread across a given space.

    They are designed to work in tandem and maximize coverage, thereby enhancing the overall performance of the WiFi network.

    Frontier FiOS Compatibility

    Ensuring that your router is compatible with Frontier FiOS is important so that you can maximize the internet speed and avoid service interruptions and untimely delays.

    Routers that are not compatible with FiOS service can lead to slow internet and service interruptions.

    You can do this by checking the website of Frontier FiOS or calling them to get a list of compatible routers, then use it to decide which router fits your needs.

    Additionally, the manual of the router you purchase may contain a list of internet service providers that it is compatible with.

    Mesh System vs Traditional Routers

    In contrast to a traditional WiFi router that uses a centralized system, mesh routers rely on a decentralized system.

    When choosing a router, it is important to consider your home or office setup and determine the type of router that is best suited for your needs.

    When using a traditional WiFi router, a single unit is typically placed in one room of a house, and all devices connect to it.

    However, this setup can result in weaker internet coverage, as the network signal from the single unit may not be able to reach all rooms effectively.

    To address the problem of weaker internet coverage, the Mesh system was developed.

    Unlike traditional WiFi routers, the Mesh system uses WiFi nodes that acknowledge the fact that a single router cannot be optimally positioned to provide a strong internet connection for everyone.

    In the Mesh system, one device is designated as the primary router, while other units are strategically positioned throughout the entire area and function as satellites.

    Despite the presence of multiple units, the network appears seamless and unified to the user.

    Number Of Connected Devices & Online Activities

    Other factors to consider when buying a router that is compatible with Frontier FiOS are the number of online activities and connected devices that use WiFi at the same time.

    If many devices are using the WiFi network at once, you can experience unnecessary traffic on your network which might affect the quality of the content you are watching.

    If there is network traffic, streaming high-resolution videos for example will become challenging. To avoid such issues, you should buy a router that can support the number of devices in your household.

    Frontier FiOS Technology Explained

    The Fiber optic internet offered by Frontier can send internet data at 70% of the speed of light. This insane speed is enabled through the fiber optic cables which support connection speeds of approximately 1,000 Mbps.

    Frontier FiOS achieves faster internet speeds compared to a standard internet cable setup because the fiber optic cables use modulated light signals which travel faster and clearer over long distances, compared to coaxial cables.

    The introduction of fiber optic technology is fairly new to residential internet systems, however, the technology has been around for many years.

    For example, in 1988, the first fiber optic cable was constructed across the Atlantic to improve telephone communication speeds.

    Frontier FiOS offers significant benefits compared to standard DSL cables. These include faster upload and download speeds, improved gaming and streaming capability, increased connection reliability, and minimal disruption.

    Router For Frontier FiOS FAQs

    What Routers Are Compatible With Frontier FiOS?

    In theory, all routers are compatible with Frontier FiOS. However, connecting to the ultra-fast Frontier FiOS network requires a router that can handle such speeds.

    Routers that are equipped with the WiFi 6 standard at minimum, along with advanced features such as MU-MIMO, OFDMA and Beamforming, are the most compatible.

    Some of the routers that are compatible with Frontier FiOS include TP-Link Archer AX73, NETGEAR Nighthawk AXE11000, TP-Link AX3000, ASUS RT AX82U, Eero Mesh System, Gryphon Router, and Linksys MR7350.

    Does Frontier FiOS Technology Use Modems? 

    No, Frontier FiOS technology uses a device known as an ONT box but does not use a modem. Therefore, you do not need to buy a modem to set up a Frontier FiOS router.

    Can I Use A Third-Party Router With Frontier FiOS?

    Yes, you can use a third-party router with Frontier FiOS. This is because there is no ISP compatibility factor when it comes to WiFi routers, unlike cable modems, so you can pair a third-party device of your choice with it.

    You just need to ensure that the associated technologies and hardware specifications are effective enough to work well with the Frontier internet plan that you are subscribed to.

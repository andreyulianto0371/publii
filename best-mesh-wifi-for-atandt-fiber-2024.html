    WiFi SpeedÂ &Â CoverageÂ 

    WiFi speed is influenced by the WiFi standard, also known as the WiFi protocol. For mesh systems, the available WiFi standards are WiFi 5, 6, 6E, and 7.

    WiFi 5 (802.11ac) mesh systems operate best on AT&T Fiber plans that are 500 Mbps and below. Mesh systems built with WiFi 5 will be compatible with all internet-using devices and should be affordably priced.

    WiFi 6 (802.11ac) and WiFi 6E standards offer faster WiFi speeds compared to WiFi 5, with both capable of handling gigabit AT&T Fiber plans.

    WiFi 6E mesh systems include an additional 6 GHz band for more reliable and faster internet speeds.

    WiFi 7 (802.11be) mesh systems (such as the TP-Link Deco BE85) are starting to be released but there are not many WiFi 7 compatible internet devices available, meaning the benefits such as peak speeds of 40 Gbps cannot be widely experienced within households yet.

    In terms of coverage, mesh systems should deliver a coverage between 2,000 to 5,500 sq. ft. We, therefore, recommend mesh systems are worthwhile investments for properties with square footage of at least 3,000.

    Do note that mesh system coverage is impacted by the WiFi frequency bands and is a trade-off with speed.

    The 2.4 GHz band offers the slowest speed but broadest coverage, while the 5 GHz or 6 GHz bands offer faster speeds but weakest coverage.

    FeaturesÂ 

    Purchasing a third-party mesh system to connect to your AT&T Fiber internet plan enables you to access the benefits of advanced features such as MU-MIMO, OFDMA, and Beamforming. These features are explained below.

    For households with multiple device connections, the MU-MIMO feature enables reliable and smooth internet connectivity for all devices.

    MU-MIMO allows devices to quickly communicate with the router and acquire access to the signal with limited interruption.

    If you are a high-consumption internet household, OFDMA reduces congestion on the network by enabling more data carriers to operate within a frequency channel.

    This prevents overload and bottlenecks, enabling users to stream, a video conference or game with minimal lag.

    Beamforming technology enables the antennas to achieve a more precise signal while also reducing the level of interference. This leads to a faster, more reliable internet connection throughout the home.

    Number Of Supported DevicesÂ 

    Given the demands of modern-day households where the majority have many devices connected to the internet at the same time, mesh systems need to deliver an adequate number of connected devices that suits your householdâs needs.

    Most mesh systems can connect a minimum of 75 devices, but some systems can connect well over 150 devices.

    To find out the number of connected devices you need, we advise totaling the number of internet devices you have and adding a 10% to 20% premium to reach the actual figure required.

    Do note that the number of connected devices is theoretical in that it does not consider the usage type.

    A mesh system offering 25 connected devices is not assuming 25 gaming connections, but rather a mixture of activities.

    PortsÂ 

    The number of ports required in a mesh system is down to the number of wired connections you want in your household setup. Most mesh systems have at least two ethernet ports.

    Some mesh systems provide ethernet ports only on the router device, while others provide ethernet ports on the router and satellites, enabling more wired connections.

    You may consider a mesh system with more ports if you have multiple consoles or computers that benefit from wired connections.

    Mesh WiFi System vs WiFi ExtenderÂ 

    When deciding between a mesh WiFi system and a WiFi extender, there is a trade-off between the stronger speed and coverage offered by mesh, compared to the reduced cost, easy setup, and decreased energy consumption offered by WiFi extenders.

    If your goal is to expand the existing speed of your AT&T Fiber plan reliably to every corner of your home and you reside in a property that is greater than 2,000 sq ft, investing in a mesh system is the obvious choice.

    Through creating a unified network and AI-assisted communication between the router and satellites, mesh systems enable users to log in to the network once and then have their connectivity managed based on their location in the home, to ensure they are always connected to the nearest WiFi access point.

    However, if you have a smaller property and donât want to completely change your household setup, investing in a WiFi extender is the better choice.

    WiFi extenders are affordable and easy to set up. Do note however that WiFi extenders can consume up to 50% of the network bandwidth so you may notice a dip in overall performance when connected to the main router.

    FAQs

    Can I Use A Mesh WiFi System With AT&T Fiber?Â 

    You can use a mesh WiFi system with AT&T Fiber, and pretty much all mesh systems are compatible with the ISP.

    Although AT&T Fiber requires you to use the rented gateway device, you can simply put it in bridge mode so that it acts as the modem which can then connect to the third-party router of the mesh system.

    How To Install A Mesh WiFi With AT&T Fiber?Â 

    First place the AT&T gateway in bridge mode and then connect the gateway to the mesh router using an ethernet cable via WAN ports.

    Once connected restart the gateway and boot the mesh router to proceed with the setup.

    Once the router is set up, you can then connect the nodes to the router and place them strategically within your home. Â 

    Do I Need To Keep Using The AT&T Gateway?Â 

    Yes, as part of AT&Tâs terms of agreement, you need to main the gateway rental even if you then purchase a mesh WiFi system.

    The mesh WiFi system takes over the router functionality of the gateway, but the gateway needs to be put into bridge mode to maintain the modem functionality.

    Should I Get A Mesh WiFi System Or A WiFi Router?Â 

    This is down to personal preferences and also the size of your property. For properties larger than 2,000 sq. ft that are struggling with WiFi coverage, we would recommend investing in a mesh WiFi system.

    For smaller properties that want to improve speed and connectivity, we recommend a quality third-party AT&T router will suffice.

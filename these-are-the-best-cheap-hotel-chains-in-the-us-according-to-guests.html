    These Are the Best Cheap Hotel Chains in the U.S., According to Guests

    Andre Yulianto January 12, 2024

    With so many different hotels, motels, and roadside inns scattered throughout the country, large chains lure travelers off highways with the promise of familiarity and at least some minimum standards. But not all chains provide the same service, amenities, and overall satisfaction—even those in the same relative price range.

    In J.D. Power’s recently released 2023 North America Hotel Guest Satisfaction Index Study, hotel chains are ranked based on feedback from guests. Here are what customers determined are the best cheap hotel chains in the United States.

    The Best Cheap Hotel Chains in the U.S., According to Guests

    To come up with these rankings, the team at J.D. Power surveyed people who were hotel guests between May 2022 and May 2023, asking them to weigh in on a hotel chain’s performance in six areas:

    • Communications and connectivity

    • Food and beverage

    • Guest room

    • Hotel facility

    • Staff service

    • Value for price.

    The survey data was used to rank chain hotel properties across nine categories, based on their overall customer satisfaction rating—with 1,000 being a perfect score. The average rating for economy chain hotels is 558 out of 1,000. Here’s how a variety of budget chains ranked:

    1. Americas Best Value Inn
      Score: 637

    2. Microtel by Wyndham
      Score: 637

    3. SureStay
      Score: 618

    4. Howard Johnson by Wyndham
      Score: 610

    5. Travelodge by Wyndham
      Score: 580

    6. Studio 6
      Score: 578

    7. Red Roof Inn
      Score: 576

    8. Super 8 by Wyndham
      Score: 560

    9. Days Inn by Wyndham
      Score: 558

    10. Knights Inn
      Score: 551

    11. Motel 6
      Score: 533

    12. Econo Lodge
      Score: 517

    13. Rodeway Inn
      Score: 499

    14. OYO
      Score: 487

    The best economy extended-stay hotel chains

    The J.D. Power survey also asked guests about their experiences at economy extended-stay hotel chains. The average satisfaction score for this category is 585 out of a possible 1,000 points. Here’s how the three chains in this segment fared:

    1. WoodSpring Suites
      Score: 609

    2. Extended Stay America
      Score: 583

    3. InTown Suites
      Score: 579

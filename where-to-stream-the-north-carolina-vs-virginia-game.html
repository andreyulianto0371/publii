    Where to Stream the North Carolina Vs. Virginia Game

    Andre Yulianto January 4, 2024

    North Carolina and Virginia are scheduled to play in an Atlantic Coast Conference game on Saturday, Oct. 21, 2023 at Kenan Stadium in Chapel Hill, North Carolina. The game is scheduled for kickoff at 6:30 p.m. ET.

    North Carolina is ranked No. 10 in The Associated Press poll and is 6-0 overall and 3-0 in the ACC. Most recently, the UNC Tar Heels beat the Miami Hurricanes 41-31 on Oct. 14. Virginia, on the other hand, is 1-5 overall and 0-2 in the ACC. On Oct. 7, UVA defeated William & Mary 27-13. Entering Saturday, UNC leads the all-time series 65-58-4 against Virginia.

    When and where to stream North Carolina vs. UVA

    North Carolina Tar Heels vs. Virginia is scheduled to be broadcast by The CW at 6:30 p.m. ET on Saturday, Oct. 21. The CW broadcasters are scheduled to be Tom Werme (play-by-play), James Bates (analyst), and Treavor Scales (sideline reporter). But if you don’t have cable or satellite TV, there’s an online live streaming alternative, and the main option is YouTube TV.

    The best streaming service to watch UNC Tar Heels vs. UVA

    There aren’t many streaming options to watch UNC play Virginia on The CW, but YouTube TV offers the network. YouTube TV has 100 live TV channels, one of which is The CW, so you’ll be able to watch the North Carolina Tar Heels vs. Virginia game with a subscription. YouTube TV also includes six household accounts, three simultaneous streams, and unlimited DVR space.

    YouTube TV provides 30 minutes of viewing with no commitment; after that, there’s a three-month introductory rate of $52.99/month. After the introductory period, the service costs $72.99/month.

    The YouTube TV sports channels include ABC, ACC Network, Big Ten Network, CBS, CBS Sports Network, ESPN, ESPN2, ESPNews, Fox, FS1, FS2, NBC, NBCSN, NFL Network, SEC Network, and The CW.
